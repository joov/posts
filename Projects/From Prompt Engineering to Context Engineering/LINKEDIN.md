# LinkedIn Abstract: From Prompt Engineering to Context Engineering

## Main LinkedIn Post

Is Prompt Engineering dead? 🪦

The real competitive advantage in AI is shifting somewhere else:

➡️ **Context engineering**

In other words:

Prompting is like giving a new employee a task. 📝

Context engineering is like setting up their full workspace. 🧰📂🔑

You give them:

- the right documents 📄
- the right tools 🛠️
- the right access rights 🔐
- the right background knowledge 🧠
- the right people to talk to 👥

That is exactly what modern AI systems need.

Because in 2026, most failures are no longer caused by a "bad prompt" ❌

They happen because the model sees the wrong information...
too much information...
or the right information at the wrong time. ⚠️

That is why I believe this shift matters so much for leaders:

💡 The model is increasingly a commodity.
💡 The **context architecture** is the differentiator.

If two companies use the same model, the one with the better context wins. 🏆

Not because it has the smarter prompt.
Because it has the smarter system around the model.

That means the investment focus should move from:

- prompt templates ✍️
- model access 🤖
- isolated experiments 🧪

to:

- context design 🏗️
- persistent knowledge 🧠
- tool integration 🔌
- instrumentation and observability 📊

If you are still asking
"How do we write the perfect prompt?"

you may already be optimizing the wrong layer. 👇

I wrote this piece to unpack why **prompt engineering is not dead - it got promoted**.

Curious: how is your organization thinking about context today? Are you still optimizing prompts, or already designing the full information environment around AI? 💬
