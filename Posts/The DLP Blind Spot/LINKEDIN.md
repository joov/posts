# LinkedIn Abstract — The DLP Blind Spot

---

## Main Post

Can you tell me right now which AI tools your employees used in the last 30 days — and what data went into them? 🔍

If not, you have a blind spot. Here's what it looks like.

---

Samsung authorized ChatGPT for internal use in March 2023.

Three weeks later, engineers had pasted proprietary source code, hardware testing sequences, and internal meeting notes into the chatbot.

The data was gone. No file moved. No alert fired. ✅ DLP reported all-clear.

Because the data didn't leave through the door.
It left through a conversation. 💬

---

This is the DLP blind spot — and almost every AI-first company has it right now.

Traditional DLP was built for a world where data moves in containers: files, attachments, USB drives. It scans for patterns: credit card numbers, SSNs, the word "CONFIDENTIAL."

That world no longer exists.

---

**3 blind spots your DLP probably can't see:** 👇

💬 **The Prompt**
77% of employees paste data into GenAI tools.
82% do it via personal accounts — no audit trail, no coverage.
GenAI is now the #1 channel for corporate data leaving sanctioned environments.

🧠 **The Context**
Your M&A strategy doesn't match a regex.
Your competitive intelligence has no keyword to flag.
Pattern matching was never built to detect *meaning*.

🤖 **The AI Agent**
AI agents retrieve data, summarize it, pass it on — all without a human initiating a single step.
Microsoft Copilot silently exfiltrated enterprise data via a single malicious email. The security stack reported all-clear. For four weeks.

---

And then there's the permission trap. 🪤

Your employees are being asked to approve AI tool permissions they don't understand.
"May I access your files?" "May I send this email?"

They click Allow. Again and again.
Not out of carelessness — out of consent fatigue.

96.8% of AI agent tools carry no warning about what they actually do. 😬

---

The fix isn't more training. It's architecture.

🔑 Deploy sanctioned AI first — it reduces Shadow AI demand by 60–80% overnight.
📊 Measure what matters: sensitive prompt rate trends, not raw alert counts.
🛠️ Know the difference between what DLP can do today vs. what's still maturing.

---

DLP is not dead. But the version watching file transfers is watching the wrong building. 🏢
