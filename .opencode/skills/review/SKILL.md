## What I do

Read the project's POST.md and review it against three criteria:

### 1. Logical structure
- Does the article follow a clear narrative arc?
- Does each section build on the previous one?
- Are transitions between sections smooth?
- Does the conclusion deliver on the promise of the introduction?

### 2. Every technical term explained
- Identify every technical term, acronym, or jargon used in the article
- Check whether each term is explained on first use (inline or in context)
- Flag any term that a technically interested but non-expert reader would not understand
- Suggest a brief explanation for each flagged term

### 3. Technical concepts mentioned out of context
- Flag any technical concept that is mentioned but not connected to the article's argument
- Identify "name drops" -- references to people, tools, or frameworks that add no value
- Check that every example or reference serves the article's core message
- Flag anything that assumes knowledge the target audience likely doesn't have

## Output format

Present the review as a structured report with three sections (one per criterion). For each issue found:
- Quote the relevant passage
- Explain the problem
- Suggest a fix

End with an overall assessment: ready to publish, needs minor edits, or needs significant rework.

## When to use me

Used after the post is written (POST.md exists). This is the quality gate before publishing.
