## What I do

I check the plan of the curren post. This is done by reviewing PLAN.md. Write the results to CHECK.md

### Consistency of the plan

- does the plan follow a locical line of thoughts
- is the argumentation MECE?

### Readability

- are there ideas or items which distract from the main focus
- Is the article overly complex
- Should it be devided into several articles with distinct headers

### Usability

- Are the results and recommendations actionable
- Does the plan contain interesting and valueable information
- is the target group defined and is the plan consistent to this target group

### Source Verification

After writing the plan, verify every factual claim and statistic against its cited source:

1. Fetch or search each source URL and confirm the specific claim, number, or quote actually appears there
2. Check attribution accuracy — distinguish between primary sources (original research/reports) and secondary sources (blogs, articles citing others)
3. Note any framing nuances — e.g. a statistic scoped to a specific subset that the plan uses more broadly
4. Flag vendor analysis or marketing content clearly — it can be used, but should be attributed as "vendor analysis" rather than independent research
5. Add a **Source Verification Notes** section at the bottom of `PLAN.md` documenting the status of each claim:
   - **CONFIRMED** — claim verified as stated
   - **CONFIRMED with note** — claim verified but requires a framing caveat when writing
   - **UNVERIFIED** — could not confirm; mark for removal or replacement before writing
   - **INCORRECT** — claim does not match source; correct or remove it

Used after the plan is made
