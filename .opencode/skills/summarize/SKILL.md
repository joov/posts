## What I do

- Read the project's `POST.md`
- Create a short LinkedIn version of the post that works as an abstract/teaser
- Keep the summary clearly connected to the original article's core thesis
- Make it more attention-grabbing than the post itself, with a touch more clickbaiting, but do not become spammy or misleading
- Use plenty of emojis where they help scannability and energy
- Create 2-3 alternative hook lines for the opening
- Save the output as `LINKEDIN.md` in the project folder

## Output structure

The output should include:

1. **Main LinkedIn Post**
   - 1 strong opening hook
   - short punchy paragraphs
   - lots of emojis
   - 1 clear core insight
   - 1 invitation to engage (question or call to comment)
   - Want to know more? See comment for link to the complete article
   - 3-5 hashtags at the end

2. **Alternative Hooks**
   - 2-3 additional first-line options
   - each should be more curiosity-driven than the article title

## Style

- Written for LinkedIn, not Medium
- Short, punchy, scroll-stopping
- Slightly more clickbaity than the article, but still credible
- Use line breaks generously
- Prefer plain language over technical detail
- Keep the post self-contained
- Make the reader curious enough to click through to the full article

## When to use me

Used after the post is written. This is the repurposing step for LinkedIn distribution.
